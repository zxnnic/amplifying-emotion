Make it Happier! Discretizing and Amplifying Happiness in Animated Faces

Jessica Bo and Zixin Zhao
CSC2521 Course Project

Introduction

Modeling human expression within facial animation is a complex task as it involves the interplay between a multitude of facial muscles. The Facial Action Coding System (FACS) (Ekman & Friesen, 1978) has become a popular tool for encoding facial movements based on the musculoskeletal system, and offers a systematic approach to model emotions on human faces. Despite the advances in automated facial animation, there is still a reliance on human actors and animators to create realistic expressions on animated faces. Previous works in animation focus on distinguishing distinct emotions at two levels: neutral and showing extreme emotion. Prior literature shows that there are six main emotions (happy, sad, angry, disgust, surprise, fear) (SOURCE) and we focused our works on happiness after some pilot testing. Our project aims to explore the possibility of identifying the quantifiable levels of expressed emotions on human faces beyond the neutral and activated. Additionally, explore methods of amplifying emotions on different facial models like Apple Face (SOURCE) and JALI's ValleyGirl (SOURCE).
